Cookie Policy

Information on the cookies used by the euriskosrl.it site, in line with EU Regulation 2016/679 and the Italian Data Protection Authority guidelines of June 10, 2021.

Last updated: April 22, 2026 · Version 1.0

What cookies are

Cookies are small text files that the websites you visit send to your device, where they are stored to be sent back to the same sites on subsequent visits. They are used for IT authentication, session monitoring and to store information about user activity.

Types of cookies used

Technical cookies (always active)

These cookies are necessary for the proper functioning of the site and do not require user consent.

Cookie namePurposeDurationType
eurisko_consent Stores the user's choices regarding cookies 12 months First-party

Analytics cookies (optional, subject to consent)

At present the site does not use third-party analytics cookies. Should an analytics service be activated in the future (e.g. Vercel Analytics, Plausible, Matomo or Google Analytics in anonymized mode), this policy will be updated accordingly and the activation will be subject to the user's consent expressed via the cookie banner.

Marketing cookies (optional, subject to consent)

The site does not use profiling or marketing cookies.

Third-party services

The site relies on the following third-party services that may set technical or operational cookies on the user's device:

  • Vercel Inc. — hosting provider. For details: Vercel Privacy Policy.
  • Fontshare (ITC Group) — provider of the web fonts used. For details: Fontshare Privacy Policy.
  • Formspree Inc. — service used for the contact form submission. For details: Formspree Privacy Policy.

How to manage your preferences

You can change your cookie preferences at any time using the banner that appears on first visit or, later, by accessing the cookie settings via the link in the page footer.

You can also manage cookies directly in your browser:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

Disabling technical cookies may affect the proper functioning of the site.
